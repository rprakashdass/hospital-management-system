Introduction to Heart Disease Prediction Model

The Lehme Model is a state-of-the-art prediction algorithm designed to assist in diagnosing health conditions based on patient symptoms and clinical data. This model leverages advanced machine learning to analyze input data and provide accurate predictions for various diseases.

Parameters Needed:

  • Height (cm): Patient's height in centimeters (50-250 cm).
  • Weight (kg): Patient's weight in kilograms (20-200 kg).
  • Temperature (°C): Body temperature in degrees Celsius (30-45 °C).
  • Heart Rate (bpm): Beats per minute (40-200 bpm).
  • Cholesterol (mg/dL): Cholesterol level in milligrams per deciliter (100-300 mg/dL).
  • Blood Sugar (mg/dL): Blood sugar level in milligrams per deciliter (50-500 mg/dL).
  • Systolic Pressure: Blood pressure's upper reading (80-200 mmHg).
  • Diastolic Pressure: Blood pressure's lower reading (50-120 mmHg).
  • Existing Conditions: Diabetes, Hypertension, High cholesterol, Asthma.
  • Family History: Indication of heart disease in family history (Yes/No).
  • Smoking Status: Never, Former, or Current smoker.
  • Lab Status: Results such as high blood sugar, high cholesterol, low iron, or normal.
  • Symptoms: Chest pain, dizziness, fatigue, nausea, palpitations, or shortness of breath.

How It Works:

The input parameters are processed and scaled to match the expected input format of the Lehme Model. The model predicts a disease label, mapping it to a known health condition based on the provided data.
